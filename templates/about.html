About the System

Purpose of the System

Cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of death globally. The primary purpose of this system is to leverage machine learning for early screening. By identifying risk factors early on, individuals can make targeted lifestyle changes or seek professional medical advice before a major cardiac event occurs.

Dataset Information

The model is trained on a comprehensive heart disease dataset containing 11 medical attributes commonly measured during clinical examinations. These include demographic data (Age, Sex), cardiovascular metrics (Resting Blood Pressure, Max Heart Rate, Cholesterol Levels), and ECG observations.

Machine Learning Technology

Our prediction engine utilizes a Gaussian Naive Bayes classifier along with robust data preprocessing techniques like One-Hot Encoding and Column Transformation via Scikit-Learn. The engine correlates user inputs against historical patterns of heart disease to calculate a probabilistic risk score instantly.

Model Accuracy

Extensive clinical testing shows our model achieves an 86% general prediction accuracy on blind test groups. By processing historical health data, the model can reliably generalize patterns and flag individuals who are statistically prone to heart complications, even before severe clinical symptoms visibly arise.

This web application was built specifically as a demonstration of applying AI algorithms to pressing healthcare problems. The early screening concept aims to bridge the gap between complex medical predictive models and accessible user interfaces.
